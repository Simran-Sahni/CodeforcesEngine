Orac and Modelstime 3  256 There are $$$n$$$ models in the shop numbered from $$$1$$$ to $$$n$$$, with sizes $$$s_1, s_2, \ldots, s_n$$$.Orac will buy some of the models and will arrange them in the order of increasing numbers (i.e. indices, but not sizes).Orac thinks that the obtained arrangement is beatiful, if for any two adjacent models with indices $$$i_j$$$ and $$$i_{j+1}$$$ (note that $$$i_j < i_{j+1}$$$, because Orac arranged them properly), $$$i_{j+1}$$$ is divisible by $$$i_j$$$ and $$$s_{i_j} < s_{i_{j+1}}$$$.For example, for $$$6$$$ models with sizes $$$\{3, 6, 7, 7, 7, 7\}$$$, he can buy models with indices $$$1$$$, $$$2$$$, and $$$6$$$, and the obtained arrangement will be beautiful.
